A pilot-first private airpark on Georgia’s Black Sea

1000 m runway strip, ICAO-compliant apron with 20 GA stands, 15×20 m private hangars, a boutique hotel with an ATC tower, and an optional residential cluster on the land side.

Runway strip ~1000 × 100 m 20 GA stands Private hangars 15×20 m Hotel + ATC tower

Masterplan Overview (12 ha)

The property totals 12 hectares. 10 ha is dedicated exclusively to the runway strip (grass in Phase 1). The remaining 2 ha is the airside/land-side development area: it includes an east block (≈40 × 70 m) for the hotel + ATC tower, pool/yard, parking, and the compact residential cluster, and a 200 × 70 m apron block that hosts the apron with GA stands, the row of private, common and maintenance hangars, and the fuel station.

  • • Runway strip: 1000 m × 100 m (grass), upgrade-ready
  • • Apron block: ≈200 m × 70 m with taxi/stand system (ICAO Code B)
  • • East block (≈40 m × 70 m): hotel + ATC, pool/yard, parking, residential cluster

Hangars & Optional Homes

Air-side: a single row of private 15×20 m hangars fronts the apron. A 30×20 m Maintenance hangar and a 30×20 m Common hangar are reserved for later phases.

Land-side: if requested, buyers can add a home in the residential cluster—eight houses (8×7 m) positioned just north of the hotel, between the hotel block and the yard/pool.

Apron & Airside Operations

The 50 m depth supports two parallel GA stand rows, a mid (longitudinal) taxiway and a south service taxiway, with a safety strip toward the runway. There is one mid-apron runway exit; runway holding points are planned at the east and west ends.

  • • 20 GA stands (T-marks), center-to-center spacing ≈ 12 m
  • • Gaps between adjacent stands ≈ 4 m
  • • Mid taxiway width ≈ 10.5 m (Code B); end taxiways (W & E) ≈ 10.5 m each
  • • Remaining depth ≈ 12.5 m for edge margins / curves / misc.
  • • Fuel station is on the air side adjacent to the hotel; its service road runs east–west along the south of the hotel building and enters the apron from there.

East Block — Hotel, ATC Tower, Residential Zone & Yard

Boutique hotel (approx. 31×26 m footprint) integrated with an ATC tower. To the north is the yard/pool and green area; to the east, a 7 m × 50 m public parking lane. A one-way service road runs along the south edge of the hotel—this road provides fuel-truck access to the apron and the adjacent fuel station.

Residential zone: a compact cluster of eight single-family homes (8×7 m) immediately north of the hotel/yard. Homes front an internal lane within the 40 m × 70 m east block envelope, with short setbacks and pedestrian links to the yard/pool and hotel. Utilities and vehicle access are planned from the east-block road.
